Join the Shared Use Initiative

Who we are

We are a group of government, public, private, nonprofit and faith-based entities participating in Maricopa County's Shared Use Initiative. Together, we can help ensure more safe and accessible places to gather are available to our community.

What we do

Many Maricopa County residents, especially children, have limited access to safe spaces to play and gather. Limited access to recreational spaces can result in poor physical and mental health. The Maricopa County Shared Use Initiative brings together community members, schools, state and local government officials, businesses, foundations and others dedicated to unlocking spaces, supporting communities and creating healthier environments in our county and beyond.

The people behind the magic

From participating sites to Maricopa County Department of Public Health staff and volunteers, the Shared Use Initiative is made possible by a large community.

How we work

There are currently six participating Shared Use sites located across Maricopa County. These sites engage their community members through extended facilities hours, facilities rentals, public community events, and by providing safe spaces for gathering.

Technology

With the help of technology, we can connect communities within Maricopa County to nearby sites offering engaging activities.

Social

Shared Use spaces offer a safe and affordable option for community members to gather and play. By utilizing existing space, and repurposing facilities to meet the needs of a community, we are able to cut back on new facility construction within Maricopa County and the surrounding area.

Community

With the support of the Shared Use Initiative from organizations and community members, we are able to build awareness for existing sites, and prompt for the involvement of new sites.
